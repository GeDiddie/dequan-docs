Every token carries a **Token Quality Score (TQS)** — a number from 0 to 100 that summarises the underlying risk and quality picture into one signal.

Hover over any token in the Pump Zone and a card pops out to the side showing the TQS alongside other quick data bits. You see the score before you ever open a chart.

## Where it shows up

- **The token glyph** in the Pump Zone carries the TQS badge
- **The chart palette** is keyed off TQS — HOT renders in gold candles, WARM in cinematic teal/red, COOL in ice-blue
- **The Sniper List** shows TQS as a sortable column
- **The candle drawer** highlights TQS changes as they happen

## Why a single number?

Most safety tooling buries you in a dozen separate metrics — top-10 holders %, dev hold %, snapshot snipers, bundlers, mint authority status. All of them are useful, none of them are decisions.

A score is a decision. **TQS is the decision-grade summary** of that whole signal cluster, calibrated against historical outcomes.

You can still inspect every component — see the [Token Detail Tabs](/charts/token-detail-tabs) — when you want the full breakdown. TQS is the headline; the tabs are the article.

## What goes into it

We deliberately don't publish the exact weighting. The score is one of Dequan's research outputs and the calibration evolves as the meta evolves — what predicted "HOT" six months ago doesn't predict it now.

What we *do* commit to:

- TQS is **never paid promotion**. There is no way to buy a higher score.
- TQS is **never adjusted on request**. Not for projects, not for influencers, not for anyone.
- TQS is **not biased by venue** — the score doesn't reward tokens for using a particular DEX or launchpad.
- TQS is **based purely on observable on-chain and market data**. No off-chain reputation inputs, no Twitter sentiment, no manual flagging.

## Live updates

TQS is a streaming score, not a snapshot. As conditions change — concentration shifts, dev moves, bonding curve advances, trade activity profile evolves — the score updates and the chart palette shifts with it.

Watching a chart go from teal to gold while you're on it is a real signal. Something about the token's profile just improved.

## TQS is not a buy signal

We have to say this explicitly because someone will misread the docs otherwise.

TQS is a **risk assessment**. A HOT token can still rug. A COOL token can still print. The score tells you what the conditions look like; it does not tell you whether to trade.

Used correctly, TQS narrows the field of consideration. It does not replace the trade decision.
