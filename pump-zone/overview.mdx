## The premise

Token launches are visual events. A coin doesn't just "go up" — it accelerates, breaks through, leaves a trail, attracts a crowd, then either explodes or burns out. **You can see that.** A list cannot.

Every Solana trading tool today renders the market as a **table**. Rows of mint addresses, columns of percent-change. The interface throws away every cue our visual system is good at: motion, trajectory, density, formation, contrast.

The Pump Zone exists because we asked one question: *what would it look like if a feed could actually breathe?*

## The mental model

The Pump Zone is a **live field**. Tokens are objects on the field. Their position is meaningful. Their motion is meaningful.

<Frame>
  <img src="/images/pump-zone-screenshot.png" alt="The Pump Zone live field — tokens positioned by market cap (horizontal) and velocity (vertical)" />
</Frame>

- **Horizontal position = market cap.** Newly created tokens enter from the left. Maturing tokens drift right.
- **Vertical position = velocity.** Fast climbers rise. Cooling tokens sink.
- **Glow & motion = life.** A flash means a fresh trade hit. A glide means the token is moving through MC space.

Once you've used it for ten minutes, you stop "reading" the field. You start *feeling* it. The pattern recognition that experienced traders developed over years of staring at candle charts — Dequan gives that to a beginner in their first session.

## Why the field is faster than a list

A sorted list rerenders top-to-bottom on every update. The interesting token has to travel from rank 47 to rank 3 over multiple frames before you'd ever see it. Your eye is reading the wrong part of the screen.

The field doesn't sort. It **places**. A token doesn't "rise to rank 3" — it rises *visibly*, in real space, on the same frame the trade hit our edge. You don't have to scan a list. You just notice the motion.

This is also why Dequan can render the entire active market without making your laptop into a furnace: position math is constant-time per token, and the canvas only repaints regions that changed.
