The single most important thing about Dequan's safety design is that **safety is not a feature you upgrade to**. It's not gated, it's not optional, it's not Pro-tier. It runs for every user, on every token, all the time.

The thesis: we want our users to survive long enough to compound. Cheap rugs are everywhere on Solana. The best traders aren't the ones who pick the most winners — they're the ones who avoid the most catastrophic losses. So Dequan does the catching for you.

## The principle

A trading tool that lets you lose your shirt to obvious scams is **not** a fast trading tool. It is a fast losing tool.

Speed without safety compounds the wrong way. Dequan was designed by traders who lost real money to honeypots and rugs in the early days, and we are not letting that happen to our users if we can possibly help it.

This is also why we don't gate safety behind tiers. Charging more for "premium fraud protection" would be philosophically gross. Safety belongs to everyone.

## What Dequan does *not* claim

We do not claim to catch every rug. The on-chain space is adversarial and creative; new exploit patterns emerge regularly. What we do claim:

- **The known patterns are caught.** Honeypots, drain attacks, dev exits, concentration risks — all monitored.
- **The information is surfaced fast.** When something is wrong, you know within seconds, not minutes.
- **The default is "be careful."** When the platform is uncertain, it errs toward warning you.

If you're using Dequan and you see no warnings, that's not a guarantee — it's an absence of detected red flags. You should still apply judgment. But the obvious traps are filtered out before you can fall into them.
