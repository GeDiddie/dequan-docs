## What Scout's *limits* look like

Scout has limits — that's what makes it Scout — but the limits are calibrated for genuine retail trading, not artificially crippled to push upgrades.

- **Daily trade volume cap.** A maximum cumulative SOL volume per 24-hour window. Generous enough that the average active retail trader will never hit it.
- **Per-session rate limits.** Standard rate-limit boundaries to keep the platform stable for everyone.
- **Lightning Wallet balance cap.** Bounded balance suitable for retail sniping. Excess can be withdrawn back to your main wallet at any time.
- **Standard fee tier.** The base platform fee rate.
If you hit the daily volume cap regularly, that is a strong signal that you'd benefit from an invite to Sniper. We'd rather you find that out by hitting the limit than by reading marketing copy about it.

## Why is the free tier this generous?

The honest answer:

1. **The marginal cost of a Scout user is tiny.** Most of the platform's cost is fixed — data feeds, infrastructure, engineering. Adding another Scout user adds a small per-call cost that the platform absorbs comfortably.
2. **Scout users become Sniper users.** Not all of them, but the ones who grow into serious volume become the platform's high-value users. Crippling Scout would lose those traders to a competitor *before* they grew.
3. **Word of mouth is the best growth.** Free Scout users telling friends "this is actually fast" is worth more than any paid acquisition campaign.
4. **It's the right thing to do.** Charging for safety features feels gross. Charging for the feature set when the marginal cost of that feature set is near zero feels gross. We'd rather feel good about the product.

## The practical bottom line

You can sign up right now, connect a wallet, fund Lightning if you want, and have access to a tool that — measured by feature surface — costs other platforms $50–$200/month behind paywalls.

The catch is: there isn't one. Scout is the real product, and so are you.
